Modules

Modules are reusable combinations of patterns that present well-structured information with a perfect balance between copy and imagery. These best practice solutions help users find the information efficiently, encourage the conversation, and help ensure consistency across experiences.

Hero

Composition

The hero section is made up of the following elements:

  1. Lead: Page title or its category
  2. Headline - H1: A primary heading and it should be Sentence Case. The title provides a concise overview of the contents of the journey.
  3. Message: The short summary of the page.
  4. Buttons: Contains a maximum of two centered buttons. A primary action and secondary action. The action should be self-describing action verbs.
  5. Illustration: Should be composed of metaphorical imagery that relates to the content or benefit.

Best practice

  1. It should be used above the fold as it provides the concise overview of the product/solution or use case.
  2. The copy should define the requirements for the overall page. Content should drive design, structure, layout etc
  3. All first impressions should complement rather than compete with each other. Walk through the entire end-to-end journey that the users' new experience is a part of.
  4. Try not to overwhelm the audience with too much information at once. Focus on the top two to three benefits and then gradually introduce changes over time.

Illustrations

  1. Illustrations make complex ideas more accessible
  2. Illustrations should be displayed as a spot hero image that is metaphorical and designed in conjunction with UI copy
  3. Illustrations are used to efficiently convey brand personality
  4. Should not be used as decoration or without consideration.

Content guidelines

Headline - H1

  1. The ideal length is 2 lines
  2. Use the title to communicate the main benefit in an active and personalized way, for example: connect and control your products in real-time.

Message copy

  1. Try to keep the message length to less than three lines at the minimum supported size.
  2. Don't just point out what the feature or functionality is. Tell people why it's important at this point in time.
  3. Be considerate of people's time and patience. Be short and to the point, and avoid using company jargon.

Button or text link labeling

  1. Buttons or text links should always lead with a strong verb that encourages action.
  2. Link labeling need to be clear and predictable.
  3. Always write the labeling in sentence case.
  4. Avoid unnecessary words and articles such as the, an, or a.

Accessibility

  1. Include copy to supplement illustrations.
  2. Use alt text for illustrations.

Variants

  • Lead is optional, can be omitted
