Principles

Design principles are a set of guidelines our teams can follow throughout our design process in order to create more aligned experiences.

Design principles create alignment, speed up decision-making, and increase of the quality of our team's output.

Effective principles should be:

  1. Universal and specific
  2. Decision-making tools

Universal and specific

There are two types of principles: universal and specific. Source

Universal design principles

Universal principles are broadly applicable and can be used for all kinds of design.

  • Simplicity
PubNub experiences help our customers achieve goals faster, more effectively, and with less effort. We make complex tasks simple to implement, and at the same time keep the flexibility with customizable integrations.
  • Trustworthy
PubNub experiences are genuine, always honest, and transparent. We build a real-time communication platform that strengthen the trust the end users have in our customers.
  • Consistency
PubNub experiences

Specific design principles

Specific principles map directly to our website, docs, or portal and are used to align teams around a common language.

Website
  • Maintain clarity

Understand and design for our users’ main goals

Maintaining clarity in the user interface we design is key to user success and user satisfaction. Clarity allows our audience to understand what we’re trying to help them achieve. If our design has too much extraneous information, users will have trouble navigating our site. Help the user understand the message we’re trying to convey and the actions the user can take within the first few seconds of browsing. Call attention to only the core aspects of the page we want our audience to focus their attention to.

Don’t confuse our audience with extraneous information that are irrelevant to those goals. Help our audience understand what actions they can take by calling attention to only the core aspects we want them to focus on.

Portal
  • Make use of automation

Design for a minimum amount of conscious and cognitive effort

Make use of automation by designing for the least amount of cognitive effort. Figure out what the commonly practiced tasks and processes are and incorporate them into our designs whenever possible.

Docs
  • Reduce the abyss of execution

Make our audience sees how our product can help them achieve their goals

The narrower we can make the abyss of execution, the more likely our user will understand how to interact with our product. Successful design systems are designed with the user’s primary goals in mind and take into consideration the limits of human memory and the amount of attention allocated to each goal. Make our product simple enough for your users to understand how they can use it as a tool to achieve their goals.
