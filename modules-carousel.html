Modules

Modules are reusable combinations of patterns that present well-structured information with a perfect balance between copy and imagery. These best practice solutions help users find the information efficiently, encourage the conversation, and help ensure consistency across experiences.

Carousel

Composition

The carousel section is made up of the following elements:

  1. Lead: Page title or it's category
  2. Title: Sentence case. The title for this feature provides a concise overview of the feature of this product/service.
  3. Message: The short summary of the feature.
  4. Button: Contains a maximum of two centered buttons. A primary action and secondary action. The action should be self-describing action verbs.
  5. Illustration: It should be composed of metaphorical imagery that relates to the content or benefit.
  6. Left and right arrows: for navigating though the content. Users should also be able to scroll using just the keyboard.

Best practice

  1. Carousels offers an engaging interface for browsing visual items, encouraging the user to inspect the features, topics, offers, or products that are in view and to see what's next. A user can't easily jump to a certain point deep in the list, so he has to scroll through everything -- this pattern thus encourages browsing and serendipity.
  2. Each item is potentially of interest. Users will scan the slide casually without a specific need in mind.

Illustrations

  1. Illustrations tend to be similar in size and style.
  2. Illustrations make complex ideas more accessible.
  3. illustrations are used to efficiently convey brand personality.
  4. Should not be used as decoration or without consideration.

Content guidelines

Title

  1. Informative and descriptive: mainly focus on response to our users' needs.
  2. Use the title to communicate the main benefit in an active and personalized way, for example: connect and control your products in real-time.
  3. Concise and scannable: Use simple, clear language, and keep title to a single sentence.
  4. Avoid using punctuation such as periods, commas, or semicolons.

Message copy

  1. Try to keep the message length to less than three lines at the minimum supported size.
  2. Don't just point out what the feature or functionality is. Tell people why it's important at this point in time.
  3. Be considerate of people's time and patience. Be short and to the point, and avoid using company jargon.
  4. Only use industry-standard terminology when you have reason to believe it will improve understanding.

Button or text link labeling

  1. Buttons or text links should always lead with a strong verb that encourages action.
  2. Link labeling need to be clear and predictable.
  3. Always write the labeling in sentence case.
  4. Avoid unnecessary words and articles such as the, an, or a.

Interaction

  1. Rotate and display the each slide 50ms per frame.

Accessibility

  1. Use alt text for icons or illustrations.
