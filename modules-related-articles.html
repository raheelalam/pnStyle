Modules

Modules are reusable combinations of patterns that present well-structured information with a perfect balance between copy and imagery. These best practice solutions help users find the information efficiently, encourage the conversation, and help ensure consistency across experiences.

Related articles

Composition

The related articles section is made up of the following elements:

  1. Icon: thin-stroke style icon
  2. Title: A heading and it should be sentence case. Here is the place to lead the users to where we want them to read if they want to dig deeper. The idea destination would be Docs or Our platform.
  3. Message: The short summary of what it is about. Recommended length would be 2 lines.
  4. Text link: Leads to Docs or Our platform. The action should be self-describing action verbs.

Best practice

  1. It is a good idea to always include a Related articles section close the end of the page to guide the users to where we want them to read next.
  2. Customize the articles based on the page content to continue the experience cohesive. For example, if a user is on Chat page, the articles listed here should be Chat related.

Icons

  1. Icons are used to efficiently convey brand personality.
  2. Icons are used to visually communicate core parts of the features.
  3. Should not be used as decoration or without consideration.

Content guidelines

Title

  1. Informative and descriptive: mainly focus on response to our users' needs.
  2. Use the title to communicate the main benefit in an active and personalized way, for example: connect and control your products in real-time.
  3. Concise and scannable: Use simple, clear language, and keep title to a single sentence.
  4. Avoid using punctuation such as periods, commas, or semicolons.

Message copy

  1. Try to keep the message length to less than two lines at the minimum supported size.
  2. Don't just point out what the feature or functionality is. Tell people why it's important at this point in time.
  3. Be considerate of people's time and patience. Be short and to the point, and avoid using company jargon.
  4. Only use industry-standard terminology when you have reason to believe it will improve understanding.

Text link labeling

  1. Links should always lead with a strong verb that encourages action.
  2. Links need to be clear and predictable.
  3. Always write the labeling in sentence case.
  4. Avoid unnecessary words and articles such as the, an, or a.

Accessibility

  1. Use alt text for icons or illustrations.
